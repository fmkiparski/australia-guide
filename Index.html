Australia Essentials

Australien Grundlagen

Everything you need to know before you land

Alles was ihr vor der Landung wissen müsst

Visa & Immigration

Most European passport holders can apply for an eVisitor visa (subclass 651) online. Completely free, processed within hours, valid 12 months with stays up to 3 months. Apply here

Apply 2–3 weeks before travel. Digitally linked to your passport. Each family member needs a separate application.

Mobile

Prepaid SIM: AUD $30–50 for 30–40GB + calls/texts. Telstra = best coverage. eSIM: Airalo (~USD $7/3GB), Holafly (unlimited ~USD $30/7 days), or Saily (~USD $4/1GB).

Climate (Jan–Apr)

Sydney: 22–28°C. Perfect beach weather with occasional afternoon storms.
Melbourne: 15–27°C. Four seasons in one day — always pack layers.
Tropical QLD: 25–32°C. Wet season, humid, dramatic afternoon storms.
Tasmania: 12–22°C. Best time to visit. Mild and dry.
Perth/WA: 20–35°C. Hot and dry. Carry water everywhere.
NT/Uluru: Up to 38°C. Darwin is wet season — dramatic lightning and lush green.

Key Info

Currency: AUD. Tap-and-go everywhere. Tipping not expected.
Time: Sydney GMT+11 (daylight saving). +10hrs ahead of London/Berlin.
Driving: LEFT side. International permit recommended.
Emergency: Dial 000.
Power: Type I plugs (angled pins), 230V. Bring an adapter!
Sun: SPF 50+ EVERY DAY. 2–3x stronger than Europe. Reapply every 2hrs.
Water: Always carry a water bottle and stay hydrated — especially on walks and hot days. Tap water is safe and excellent everywhere.
Travel Insurance: Get comprehensive travel insurance before you leave. Australia has excellent healthcare but it is expensive for visitors. Make sure your policy covers medical expenses, trip cancellation, and adventure activities like diving and skydiving.
Bank & Cards: Notify your bank and credit card provider that you are travelling to Australia. Otherwise they may block your card for suspicious overseas transactions. Check your card’s foreign transaction fees — some cards charge 2–3% per transaction.
Powerbank: Bring a portable battery pack / powerbank. You’ll be using your phone constantly for maps, photos, and transport — especially in remote areas where charging opportunities are limited. A 20,000mAh bank will give you 3–4 full charges.
Driving Licence: Bring your home country driving licence plus an International Driving Permit (IDP). You can get an IDP from your national automobile association (ADAC in Germany, Post Office in the UK) — it takes minutes and costs around €15. Required for car hire.
Boat Licence: If you have a German (Sportbootführerschein) or English boat licence, bring it! Fabian may take you out on the water.

Fun Facts

Size: Australia is roughly the same size as mainland Europe. Sydney to Perth is further than London to Moscow.
Beaches: Over 10,000 beaches. A new one daily would take 27+ years.
Drop Bears: Locals will warn you about deadly “drop bears” — koalas that supposedly fall from trees onto unsuspecting tourists. They’re 100% fictional. It’s Australia’s favourite prank on visitors. Don’t fall for it (but maybe pretend you believe it — Aussies love it).
Coffee: Never order drip coffee. Ask for a flat white, long black, or cap. Australian coffee culture rivals Italy’s.
Slang: Australians shorten everything. Arvo = afternoon. Brekky = breakfast. Maccas = McDonald’s. Thongs = flip-flops (not underwear!).
Deadly? Despite the reputation, fatal encounters with wildlife are extremely rare. Horses injure more Australians than snakes, spiders, and sharks combined.
Oldest Civilisation: Aboriginal Australians have continuously inhabited this land for over 65,000 years — the oldest living civilisation on Earth.
Upside Down: When it’s winter in Europe, it’s summer in Australia. Christmas is celebrated on the beach with barbecues, not by the fireplace.

Wichtige Infos

Zeit: Sydney GMT+11. +10 Stunden vor London/Berlin.
Fahren: LINKS fahren. Internationaler Führerschein empfohlen.

Fun Facts

Größe: Australien ist ungefähr so groß wie Kontinentaleuropa. Sydney nach Perth ist weiter als London nach Moskau.
Strände: Über 10.000 Strände. Jeden Tag ein neuer würde 27+ Jahre dauern.
Drop Bears: Einheimische warnen vor tödlichen „Drop Bears“ — Koalas, die angeblich von Bäumen auf Touristen fallen. Komplett erfunden! Australiens Lieblingsstreich.
Kaffee: Niemals Filterkaffee bestellen. Flat White, Long Black oder Cappuccino. Australiens Kaffeekultur ist Weltklasse.
Slang: Australier kürzen alles ab. Arvo = Nachmittag. Brekky = Frühstück. Maccas = McDonald’s.
Gefährlich? Trotz des Rufs sind tödliche Wildtierbegegnungen extrem selten. Pferde verletzen mehr Australier als Schlangen, Spinnen und Haie zusammen.
Älteste Zivilisation: Aborigines bewohnen dieses Land seit über 65.000 Jahren — die älteste lebende Zivilisation der Erde.
Auf dem Kopf: Wenn in Europa Winter ist, ist in Australien Sommer. Weihnachten wird am Strand mit BBQ gefeiert.

Sydney Opera House

Book a 1-hour guided tour beneath the iconic sails. Hear the controversial story of architect Jørn Utzon, see backstage spaces, and experience an immersive digital show. Tours daily in English, German, French, Spanish, Japanese, Korean, and Mandarin. ~300 stairs. Book at sydneyoperahouse.com (from AUD $43). Check what’s on in late Feb/March 2027.

After the tour, walk east through the Royal Botanic Gardens to Mrs Macquarie’s Chair for the classic Opera House + Bridge panorama. Continue to China Doll in Woolloomooloo for modern Asian on the wharf. Best in late afternoon golden light.

Harbour Bridge Walk & The Rocks

Walk across the Sydney Harbour Bridge on the free pedestrian walkway for breathtaking views. Combine with a wander through The Rocks — Sydney’s oldest neighbourhood with cobblestone laneways. Visit the weekend Rocks Markets (Sat & Sun), pop into the Art Gallery in the Rocks and make your way to the rooftop for harbour views, then head to Squires Landing for lunch, an Aussie pale ale, or a crisp cider right on the waterfront.

Seaplane + Hermitage Walk + Watsons Bay

For a truly special Sydney day, book a seaplane scenic flight from Rose Bay (flights from ~AUD $250, 15–30 mins). Afterwards, walk the Hermitage Foreshore Walk — hidden coves, harbour views, barely any tourists. Continue to Watsons Bay — one of our favourite spots in Sydney. The views are stunning, the vibes are perfect, and the food and drinks at the Watsons Bay Boutique Hotel are excellent. Walk the South Head Heritage Trail to the lighthouse. Ferry back at sunset.

Skydiving

If not here, where? Tandem skydive over Wollongong Beach (1 hour south of Sydney) with views of the coastline, escarpment, and ocean that are absolutely breathtaking. Several operators offer jumps from 14,000 feet. If you’ve ever thought about it, Australia is the place to do it.

Rainy Day Activities

Sydney doesn’t stop when it rains. Visit the Art Gallery of NSW (free, world-class collection), the Australian Museum (natural history), Museum of Contemporary Art at Circular Quay, the Powerhouse Museum, or catch a show at the Opera House. Browse the bookshops and cafés of Surry Hills, or spend an afternoon at the Grounds of Alexandria — rain makes it even cosier.

3–5 Days: South Coast & Jervis Bay

Grand Pacific Drive → Jervis Bay

Sea Cliff Bridge (feels like driving on water), Kiama Blowhole, Hyams Beach (whitest sand in the world), dolphin kayaking. 2.5hr drive from Sydney. If you’re around our area, say hi to us in Oyster Bay or meet us at Cronulla Beach — our local beach!

Aboriginal-owned national park. Cave Beach, Green Patch. Kangaroos at dusk. Whale watching cruises from Huskisson (seasonal May–Nov).

Hampden Bridge (1898 suspension bridge), stunning countryside. Berry — antique shops, bakeries, the legendary Berry Donut Van.

Victoria & Melbourne

5–7 Day Itinerary

Melbourne Laneways & Culture

Hosier Lane street art, NGV gallery, Queen Victoria Market, Hardware Lane dinner. Coffee at Patricia or Market Lane.

St Kilda & Brighton

Brighton bathing boxes (iconic photos). St Kilda pier fairy penguins at dusk (free!). Luna Park. Acland Street cafes.

Great Ocean Road (Full Day)

A long but incredible day trip. Torquay → Bells Beach → Anglesea (kangaroos on the golf course) → Split Point Lighthouse → Lorne (lunch). Great Otway NP for wild koalas in the eucalyptus canopy. Continue to the 12 Apostles — towering limestone stacks rising from the Southern Ocean. Walk to Loch Ard Gorge and London Arch. Return via the faster inland route through Colac. Hire a car — the only practical way. Allow 12–14 hours round trip from Melbourne, departing early morning.

Yarra Valley

Domaine Chandon (sparkling wine, vineyard views), TarraWarra Estate (elegant Pinot Noir). Healesville Sanctuary for platypus! Lunch at Innocent Bystander.

Phillip Island Penguin Parade

Hundreds of little penguins waddling up the beach at sunset. Mornington Peninsula hot springs and wineries during the day. Montalto vineyard lunch.

Queensland

Reef, rainforest, Whitehaven, Sea-Doos

Riff, Regenwald, Whitehaven, Sea-Doos

5–7 Days: Tropical North Queensland

Cairns

Fly from Sydney (3hrs). Esplanade Lagoon (free!). Night markets. Sea-Doo ride: You don’t need a licence to ride a jet ski in Queensland! Book a guided Sea-Doo tour through one of the local operators for an unforgettable ride along the tropical coastline.

Great Barrier Reef

THE bucket list day. Full-day outer reef trip (significantly better than inner reef). Quicksilver, Reef Magic, or Passions of Paradise. Snorkelling and optional diving. Kaleidoscopic coral, sea turtles, reef sharks, giant clams.

Daintree Rainforest

Oldest tropical rainforest in the world (180 million years). Cable ferry crossing (watch for crocs!), Mossman Gorge swimming, Cape Tribulation where rainforest meets reef. 2hrs from Cairns.

Atherton Tablelands

Waterfalls day: Lake Eacham (volcanic crater lake), Millaa Millaa Falls (the iconic shampoo commercial waterfall), Curtain Fig Tree. Tropical fruit ice cream.

Port Douglas

Four Mile Beach, Sunday markets, Salsa Bar & Grill. Optional Daintree River crocodile cruise at sunset.

3–5 Days: Whitsundays & Gold Coast

Whitehaven Beach & Hill Inlet (Personal Highlight!)

This is one of Fabian’s absolute favourite experiences in Australia. Take a boat trip from Airlie Beach or Hamilton Island to Whitehaven Beach — 7km of pure white silica sand so fine it doesn’t retain heat, so you can walk barefoot even in the blazing sun. The water is an impossible shade of turquoise. But the real showstopper is the walk up to Hill Inlet lookout, where the tidal sandbars create ever-shifting swirls of white sand and turquoise water that look like a painting. It’s one of the most photographed spots in Australia and even more beautiful in person. Book a full-day sailing tour, or stay overnight on Hamilton Island for a more relaxed experience.

Gold Coast

Surfers Paradise for the buzz, Burleigh Heads for the charm. Walk Burleigh Head National Park trail along the rocky headland. Rick Shores for outstanding beachfront Asian dining.

Skydiving

Queensland also offers tandem skydiving over stunning beaches — Cairns, Mission Beach (with reef views!), and the Gold Coast all have operators. If not here, where else in the world?

5–7 Day Itinerary

Adelaide & Hahndorf

Adelaide Central Market (one of the Southern Hemisphere’s largest). Peel Street laneway bars. Then drive 25 minutes to Hahndorf — Australia’s oldest surviving German settlement, founded in 1839 by Lutheran settlers from Prussia. Walk the main street lined with traditional German bakeries, smallgoods shops, and pubs serving schnitzel, bratwurst, and German beer. For our German guests: it’s a surreal and charming little taste of home on the other side of the world.

Barossa Valley

Australia’s most famous wine region (1hr drive). Penfolds, Henschke, Peter Lehmann. Bold Shiraz and old-vine Grenache. Lunch at Hentley Farm.

McLaren Vale

d’Arenberg Cube (architectural wonder), Port Willunga Beach, Star of Greece clifftop lunch.

Kangaroo Island Day 1

Ferry from Cape Jervis (45min). Seal Bay (walk among sea lions), koalas, Remarkable Rocks, Admirals Arch. KI honey, spirits, cheese.

Kangaroo Island Day 2

Vivonne Bay (voted Australia’s best beach). Stokes Bay sunset. Dudley Wines dinner.

Western Australia

Wild, vast, and spectacular — from Perth to Ningaloo

Wild, weit und spektakulär

5–7 Days: Perth, Rottnest & Margaret River

Perth & Fremantle

Kings Park treetop walkway and panoramic views. Ferry to Fremantle — markets (since 1897), Little Creatures brewery, fish & chips at Cicerello’s. Fly from Sydney (5hrs).

Rottnest Island

Ferry 30min. Bike the island, meet the quokkas (world’s happiest animal!), snorkel The Basin. No cars. Book ferry early in Feb.

The Pinnacles & Lancelin Dunes

Day trip north (2hrs). Nambung NP — thousands of limestone pillars in golden sand. Lancelin for sandboarding down enormous white dunes overlooking the Indian Ocean. If you’re feeling adventurous, ask about ATV rides through the dunes — nothing beats tearing across the sand with ocean views on a BRP quad bike!

Margaret River Day 1

Drive south (3hrs). Leeuwin Estate, Vasse Felix, Voyager Estate. Cab Sav and Chardonnay country. Explore Mammoth Cave or Lake Cave.

Margaret River Day 2

Surf at Prevelly. Chocolate/nougat tasting. Cape to Cape track sections. Wills Domain lunch. Settlers Tavern live music.

Extended: WA North — Ningaloo, Whale Sharks & Remote Coast

For those with more time, the north of Western Australia is one of the most remote and spectacular coastlines on Earth. This requires at least 5–7 additional days and a sense of adventure.

Fly to Exmouth — Ningaloo Reef

Fly from Perth to Exmouth (2hrs) to reach Ningaloo Reef — a 260km fringing reef that you can literally walk to from the beach. Unlike the Great Barrier Reef, there’s no boat ride needed. Snorkel straight from the shore with manta rays, sea turtles, and tropical fish. But the ultimate experience is swimming with whale sharks (March–July) — the world’s largest fish, up to 12 metres long, gentle and magnificent. Tours run from Exmouth and Coral Bay.

Coral Bay & Turquoise Bay

Coral Bay is a tiny settlement with some of the most beautiful reef snorkelling in Australia right off the beach. Turquoise Bay in Cape Range National Park is regularly voted WA’s best beach — drift snorkel along the reef with the current for an effortless underwater experience.

Shark Bay & Shell Beach

Drive south to Shark Bay World Heritage Area. Visit Shell Beach — a beach made entirely of billions of tiny white shells, stretching 110km. See the ancient stromatolites at Hamelin Pool — living fossils that are some of the oldest forms of life on Earth (3.5 billion years old). Meet the dolphins at Monkey Mia who swim to shore every morning to be hand-fed.

Hutt Lagoon — The Pink Lake

On the way back south, stop at Hutt Lagoon near Port Gregory — a stunning bright pink lake caused by algae that produces beta-carotene. Best seen from the air or the lookout point on the highway. The colour is most vivid in summer and on sunny days — February is perfect.

5–7 Day Itinerary

Hobart & MONA

MONA (Museum of Old and New Art) — provocative, cliff-built, ferry there. Salamanca Markets (Sat). Franklin dinner. Fly 2hrs from Sydney. Feb is Hobart’s best month.

Bruny Island

Get Shucked oyster farm (freshest oysters you’ll ever eat). Bruny Island Cheese. Cape Bruny Lighthouse. Echidna spotting.

Wineglass Bay

Freycinet NP (2.5hrs). Hike to the lookout (45min), continue to the beach for a swim. Consistently ranked among the world’s most beautiful beaches.

Bay of Fires

Orange lichen on white granite, turquoise water. Walk the trail sections. Stay in Binalong Bay.

Cradle Mountain

Dove Lake Circuit (2hrs, iconic). Wombats at dusk — they’re everywhere and completely endearing. Ancient, wild landscape.

Northern Territory

Uluru, Kakadu, ancient heart

Uluru, Kakadu, das uralte Herz

5–7 Day Itinerary

Uluru Sunset

The rock changes from deep red to burnt orange to purple as the sun drops. Field of Light installation (50,000+ illuminated stems). Fly 3.5hrs from Sydney.

Uluru & Kata Tjuta

Base walk (10km, flat, 3.5hrs). Learn about Anangu culture. PM: Kata Tjuta — Valley of the Winds through narrow gorges between ancient domes.

Kings Canyon

Rim Walk (6km, 3hrs). Dramatic cliff edges, Garden of Eden waterhole. Start early (can exceed 36°C).

Darwin

Waterfront precinct. Crocosaurus Cove (swim in a cage next to a 5m croc!). Mindil Beach sunset markets.

Kakadu NP

UNESCO site. Aboriginal rock art at Ubirr (20,000+ years). Yellow Water croc cruise. Feb = wet season — dramatic landscapes, some roads closed.

Wanaka

Mount Cook

Hooker Valley Track (3hrs, NZ’s best short walk). Glacial lake, icebergs. Dark Sky Reserve stargazing.

Kaikoura

Whale watching year-round. Swim with dolphins. Fur seals on the rocks.

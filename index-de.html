Anreise & Zwischenstopps

Der Flug von Europa nach Australien ist lang — typischerweise 20–24 Stunden mit einem Zwischenstopp. Die gute Nachricht: Die meisten Routen führen durch unglaubliche Städte, die einen Zwischenstopp von 2–4 Tagen wert sind, entweder auf dem Hin- oder Rückweg. Hier sind unsere Top-Empfehlungen.

🏙️

Dubai

Weltklasse-Shopping, der Burj Khalifa, Wüstensafaris und unglaubliches Essen. Emirates fliegt direkt von den meisten europäischen Städten. Ein großartiger 2–3-tägiger Zwischenstopp.

🌴

Singapore

Ein Feinschmecker-Paradies mit atemberaubender Architektur, Gardens by the Bay und Hawker Centres. Singapore Airlines wird regelmäßig als beste Airline der Welt ausgezeichnet. Perfekt für 2–3 Tage.

🛕

Bangkok

Tempel, Street Food, schwimmende Märkte und lebendiges Nachtleben. Unglaublich günstig und endlos aufregend. Großartig für 3–4 Tage, wenn ihr die Zeit habt.

🕌

Doha

Das Museum für Islamische Kunst, der Souq Waqif, Wüstenabenteuer und hervorragendes Essen. Qatar Airways bietet einfache Verbindungen. Ein solider 2-Tage-Stopp.

Flugtipp: Diese Zwischenstopps funktionieren auf dem Hin- wie auf dem Rückweg. Bucht ein Open-Jaw-Ticket (z.B. Hinflug nach Sydney, Rückflug ab Auckland oder Cairns) für maximale Flexibilität. Qantas, Singapore Airlines, Emirates und Qatar Airways bieten alle hervorragenden Service auf dieser Strecke.

Sydney (10–14 Tage)

Sydney is where we live and where most of you will spend the majority of your time. It's a world-class city with one of the most beautiful harbours on Earth, incredible beaches, outstanding food, and a relaxed outdoor lifestyle that is hard to beat. Here are our top 20 must-do experiences.

01 Bondi to Coogee Coastal Walk

One of Sydney's most iconic walks — a 6km trail along dramatic sandstone cliffs, past stunning ocean pools and hidden beaches. Start at Bondi Icebergs, wind through Tamarama and Bronte, and finish with a drink at Coogee Pavilion. Do it in the morning for the best light and fewer crowds.

02 Sydney Opera House Tour

No trip to Sydney is complete without seeing the Opera House up close. Book a guided tour to explore the iconic sails from inside, or better yet, catch a performance — there's everything from opera to comedy. The architecture is even more breathtaking in person. Book at sydneyoperahouse.com.

03 Harbour Bridge Walk & The Rocks

Walk across the Harbour Bridge for free via the pedestrian path — the views are spectacular. Then explore The Rocks, Sydney's oldest neighbourhood, with its cobblestone laneways, weekend markets, and colonial-era pubs. Pop into the Art Gallery of NSW rooftop for harbour views, and grab a pale ale or cider at Squires Landing right on the water.

04 Manly Ferry

Take the iconic Manly Ferry from Circular Quay — the 30-minute ride through the harbour is an experience in itself. At Manly, walk the Corso to the beach, rent a surfboard, or do the Spit to Manly walk (10km, stunning). The ferry ride back at sunset is unforgettable.

05 Taronga Zoo

Take the ferry across to Taronga Zoo for close encounters with Australian wildlife against one of the world's best backdrops. See koalas, platypus, kangaroos, and the famous giraffe enclosure with harbour views. The Sky Safari cable car ride down to the wharf is a highlight.

06 Blue Mountains Day Trip

A must-do day trip, about 90 minutes west of Sydney. The Three Sisters at Echo Point, the Scenic Railway (world's steepest), and endless hiking trails through ancient eucalyptus forest. Visit Wentworth Falls and stop in Leura village for lunch. Leave early to beat the crowds.

07 Botanic Gardens → Mrs Macquarie's Chair → China Doll

Start at the Royal Botanic Gardens for a peaceful waterfront stroll, continue to Mrs Macquarie's Chair for the most photographed view of the Opera House and Bridge together, then walk along Woolloomooloo Wharf to China Doll for outstanding Asian-fusion lunch with harbour views.

08 Seaplane from Rose Bay, Hermitage Foreshore & Watsons Bay

Fly a seaplane from Rose Bay over the harbour — the views are extraordinary. Alternatively, walk the Hermitage Foreshore Track from Rose Bay to Vaucluse for secret harbour beaches. End at Watsons Bay, one of our favourite spots — great vibes, incredible views, and excellent food and drinks at the Watsons Bay Boutique Hotel.

09 Palm Beach & Barrenjoey Lighthouse

Sydney's northernmost beach and the filming location of Home & Away. Climb to Barrenjoey Lighthouse for 360-degree views of the coast and Pittwater. Pack a picnic and make a half-day of it. This area is close to our wedding venue — you'll love it.

10 Barangaroo Sunset

Head to Barangaroo Reserve in the late afternoon and find a spot along the harbour foreshore. Watch the sun set behind the Bridge while the city lights come on. It's one of Sydney's best free experiences. Grab a drink at one of the nearby waterfront bars afterwards.

11 Sydney Fish Market

The third-largest fish market in the world. Come for breakfast or an early lunch — buy the freshest sushi, oysters, and grilled fish you'll ever taste, and eat right by the water. Get there before 10am on weekends to avoid the crowds. The new market building is impressive.

12 Harbour Kayaking

Rent a kayak and paddle right past the Opera House and under the Harbour Bridge — it's an entirely different perspective of the city. Sunrise sessions are magical when the harbour is glassy and quiet. Several operators run guided tours from Lavender Bay.

13 Opera Bar Sunset

Grab a table at Opera Bar, right underneath the sails of the Opera House, and watch the sun set over the Bridge. The atmosphere is electric, the views are world-class, and the drinks are surprisingly reasonable. It's Sydney at its absolute best. Go on a Friday for live music.

14 Darling Harbour Evening

Darling Harbour comes alive in the evening with waterfront restaurants, bars, and the new harbourside precinct. It's a great area for a night out with dozens of venues to choose from — from cocktail bars to casual pubs to live music. The ICC events calendar is worth checking too.

15 Skydiving over Wollongong Beach

If not here, where? Tandem skydive from 15,000 feet over Wollongong's stunning coastline. You freefall for 60 seconds with views of the Illawarra escarpment, the Pacific Ocean, and on clear days, all the way to Sydney. An unforgettable experience — and surprisingly affordable.

16 Royal National Park Coast Track

Australia's oldest national park, just south of Sydney. The Coast Track is a stunning two-day hike (or a challenging one-day walk) along dramatic clifftops, past Wedding Cake Rock, through rainforest, and down to secluded beaches. Even a half-day section from Bundeena is spectacular.

17 Hunter Valley Wine Tasting

Australia's oldest wine region, about 2.5 hours north of Sydney. Over 150 wineries producing outstanding Semillon and Shiraz. Book a day tour or stay overnight. Highlights include Tyrrell's, Brokenback, and the cheese and chocolate shops along the route.

18 Rainy Day: Museums & Shows

If the weather turns (and it does in Sydney), head to the Art Gallery of NSW (free, world-class), the Australian Museum (natural history), the MCA (contemporary art at Circular Quay), or the Powerhouse Museum (science and design). Or book a show at the Opera House — there's always something on.

19 Watsons Bay Sundowner

We keep coming back to Watsons Bay because it's simply one of Sydney's most magical spots. Take the ferry from Circular Quay, stroll along the cliff walk to South Head, then settle in at the Boutique Hotel beer garden as the sun drops over the harbour. Fish and chips, a cold drink, golden light — perfection.

20 Secret Sydney: Hidden Gems

For those who want to go beyond the tourist trail: swim at the stunning Mahon Pool in Maroubra (a natural rock pool carved into the cliffs), explore the street art in Newtown, catch live jazz at Venue 505, or take a dawn swim at North Curl Curl rock pool. Sydney rewards curiosity.

Empfohlener 10-Tage Sydney Reiseplan

Day 1

Arrival & The Rocks

Settle into your accommodation and shake off the jet lag with a gentle walk through The Rocks and Circular Quay. Grab a coffee at a harbourside café, soak in your first views of the Opera House and Bridge, and explore the weekend markets if you arrive on a Saturday. Ease into Sydney time with dinner at a waterfront restaurant.

Day 2

Harbour Icons

Start at the Royal Botanic Gardens, walk to Mrs Macquarie's Chair for that iconic dual view, then continue to the Opera House for a guided tour. Walk across the Harbour Bridge for free via the pedestrian path. End at Squires Landing in The Rocks for a celebratory first pale ale.

Day 3

Bondi to Coogee

Do the Bondi to Coogee coastal walk in the morning when the light is golden and the crowds are thin. Stop at Bronte for a swim, and finish at Coogee Pavilion for a long, lazy lunch with ocean views. Spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach before heading to Icebergs for sunset drinks.

Day 4

Manly & Northern Beaches

Ferry to Manly, surf or swim at the beach, then explore the Corso and Manly Wharf. If you're ambitious, do the Spit to Manly walk beforehand (start early). Take the ferry back at sunset for one of Sydney's best free experiences — golden light on the harbour.

Day 5

Blue Mountains

Leave early for the Blue Mountains. See the Three Sisters, ride the Scenic Railway, and hike to Wentworth Falls. Stop in Leura for lunch at a charming café. It's a full day, but the ancient forests and vast blue valleys are worth every minute. You'll understand why they're called 'blue.'

Day 6

Watsons Bay & Rose Bay

Walk the Hermitage Foreshore Track from Rose Bay past hidden harbour beaches to Vaucluse. Take the ferry to Watsons Bay, do the cliff walk to South Head for jaw-dropping views, then settle in at the Boutique Hotel for fish and chips, beer, and one of Sydney's best sunsets.

Day 7

Taronga Zoo & Barangaroo

Ferry to Taronga Zoo in the morning — see Australian wildlife with the harbour as your backdrop. Take the Sky Safari cable car down. In the afternoon, explore Barangaroo Reserve and stay for sunset drinks at one of the waterfront bars.

Day 8

Palm Beach & Barrenjoey

Drive or bus to Palm Beach, Sydney's northernmost point. Climb to Barrenjoey Lighthouse for panoramic views, swim at the beach, and explore the area around our wedding venue. Lunch at The Boathouse at Palm Beach is a treat. This is one of our favourite parts of Sydney.

Day 9

Food, Culture & Nightlife

Start with breakfast at Bills Surry Hills, explore Newtown and its street art, visit the Art Gallery of NSW, and do some shopping in Paddington. In the evening, head to Darling Harbour for dinner and drinks — it's a fantastic area for a night out with dozens of venues.

Day 10

Fish Market, Kayak & Opera Bar

Start early at the Fish Market for the freshest seafood breakfast. Try harbour kayaking past the Opera House. Spend the afternoon at leisure — beach, shopping, or museum. Finish your Sydney adventure at Opera Bar with sunset drinks under the sails. A perfect farewell to the city.

Bondi Beach (from the south end by Icebergs), Bronte rock pool, or Mrs Macquarie's Chair for the harbour. Early risers are rewarded.

🌅 Best Sunset

Watsons Bay (our favourite — golden light over the harbour), Barangaroo Reserve, Opera Bar, or Milk Beach in Vaucluse for a secret spot.

Bondi (iconic), Manly (great surf), Shelly Beach (snorkelling), Palm Beach (peaceful), Cronulla — our local beach, say hi if you visit!

Bondi to Coogee (6km, classic), Spit to Manly (10km, diverse), Hermitage Foreshore (easy, harbour views), South Head Heritage Trail (dramatic).

Blue Mountains (Three Sisters, Wentworth Falls, Grand Canyon Track), Royal National Park Coast Track, Barrenjoey Lighthouse, Ku-ring-gai Chase.

Darling Harbour (dozens of waterfront venues, great for a night out), Newtown (live music, craft beer), Surry Hills (cocktail bars), The Rocks (historic pubs).

Rund um die Hochzeitslocation

Unsere Hochzeitslocation, Pasadena Sydney, liegt an den wunderschönen Northern Beaches bei Church Point. Diese Gegend ist einer der Geheimtipps Sydneys — ruhige Wasserwege, Nationalparks und atemberaubende Küstenlandschaft. Hier findet ihr Tipps für die Umgebung.

Church Point & Scotland Island

Church Point is a charming waterfront village on Pittwater. Take the Church Point Ferry across to Scotland Island — a tiny, car-free island community with a wonderful local pub (the Island Bar) and beautiful walking trails. It feels like stepping into another world.

Palm Beach & Barrenjoey Lighthouse

Just up the road from the venue. Iconic beach, Home & Away filming location, and the Barrenjoey Lighthouse walk with panoramic views of the ocean and Pittwater. Allow 45 minutes for the walk up.

Ku-ring-gai Chase National Park

Right on the doorstep. Drive to West Head Lookout for breathtaking views over Pittwater and Broken Bay. Walk the America Bay Track to a hidden waterfall in the bush. Aboriginal rock engravings, wildflowers, and pristine bushland.

Whale Beach

A quieter, more exclusive beach between Palm Beach and Avalon. Golden sand, turquoise water, and far fewer crowds than the famous beaches. The Whale Beach Deli is great for lunch.

Pittwater Kayaking

Rent a kayak at Church Point or Bayview and paddle the calm waters of Pittwater. You'll glide past secluded beaches, bushland shores, and houseboats. It's peaceful, scenic, and a wonderful way to spend a morning.

New South Wales

Südküste

Die Südküste von NSW ist einer der unterschätztesten Küstenabschnitte Australiens. Von dramatischen Meeresklippen bis zum weißesten Sand der Welt — perfekt für einen Roadtrip südlich von Sydney.

Wenn ihr in unserer Gegend seid, schaut bei uns in Oyster Bay vorbei oder trefft uns am Cronulla Beach — unserem Hausstrand!

Grand Pacific Drive

Start with the Grand Pacific Drive — a scenic route south from Sydney crossing the stunning Sea Cliff Bridge, which clings to the cliffs above the Pacific. Continue to Kiama, famous for its blowhole and beautiful lighthouse. The whole drive is spectacular.

Jervis Bay & Hyams Beach

Hyams Beach holds the Guinness Record for the whitest sand in the world. Jervis Bay itself is a marine paradise — crystal-clear water, dolphins, and outstanding snorkelling. Visit Booderee National Park nearby for secluded beaches and bushwalks.

Kangaroo Valley & Berry

A picturesque green valley inland from the coast with the historic Hampden Bridge and canoeing on the Kangaroo River. Nearby Berry is a charming village with excellent cafés, boutique shops, and the famous Berry Donut Van.

Byron Bay & Nordküste

Port Stephens

About 2.5 hours north of Sydney. Famous for dolphin watching (resident population of ~160 bottlenose dolphins), massive sand dunes for sandboarding, and excellent whale watching (May–November).

Cape Byron & Byron Bay

Australia's most easterly point. Walk to the Cape Byron Lighthouse for whale spotting, surf at The Pass, and soak up the laid-back bohemian vibe. The Byron hinterland has outstanding restaurants and rainforest walks.

Victoria

Melbourne & Laneways

Melbourne is Australia's cultural capital — world-famous street art in Hosier Lane, incredible coffee culture, the buzzing laneways filled with hidden bars and restaurants, and outstanding live music and comedy. It's a city that rewards exploration on foot. Don't miss the Queen Victoria Market and a tram ride through the CBD.

St Kilda Penguins

Every evening at dusk, a colony of little penguins waddles ashore at the St Kilda Pier breakwater. It's free to watch and utterly charming. Combine it with a sunset stroll along St Kilda Beach and dinner on Acland Street.

Great Ocean Road

One of the world's most scenic coastal drives. The Twelve Apostles are the showstopper, but the entire route is breathtaking — rainforest, surf beaches, and dramatic clifftop views.

Wichtig: Die Great Ocean Road ist ein LANGER Tagesausflug von Melbourne — plant 12–14 Stunden für Hin- und Rückfahrt ein. Fahrt sehr früh los (6–7 Uhr), um das Tageslicht optimal zu nutzen. Überlegt, ob ihr in Lorne oder Apollo Bay übernachten könnt.

Yarra Valley

Melbourne's wine region, just an hour east of the city. Outstanding Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, with over 80 cellar doors. Combine with the stunning Healesville Sanctuary for native wildlife encounters.

Phillip Island Penguins

The famous Penguin Parade — hundreds of little penguins emerge from the sea at sunset and waddle up the beach to their burrows. A magical experience, especially the premium viewing platforms. About 90 minutes from Melbourne.

Queensland

Cairns & tropischer Norden

Great Barrier Reef

The world's largest coral reef system and one of the natural wonders of the world. Snorkel or dive from Cairns or Port Douglas — the colours and marine life are extraordinary. Choose an outer reef trip for the best visibility and coral. A scenic helicopter flight over the reef is also unforgettable.

Sea-Doo Ride from Cairns

Explore the tropical coastline on a jet ski. No licence is needed in Queensland to ride a jet ski — book through a local operator in Cairns or Port Douglas. It's an exhilarating way to see the coast, and the warm tropical water makes it even better.

Daintree Rainforest

The world's oldest tropical rainforest (over 180 million years old). Take the cable ferry across the Daintree River, walk the boardwalks through ancient canopy, and visit Cape Tribulation where the rainforest meets the reef. Overnight stays are magical — the sounds of the jungle at night are incredible.

Atherton Tablelands & Port Douglas

The lush highlands behind Cairns with stunning waterfalls (Millaa Millaa, Zillie), crater lakes, and platypus spotting. Port Douglas is an upscale coastal town with Four Mile Beach and a more relaxed pace than Cairns. The Sunday Markets are excellent.

Whitsundays

Whitehaven Beach & Hill Inlet

This is my absolute favourite place in Australia. Whitehaven Beach is 7km of the purest white silica sand you've ever seen, and Hill Inlet — where the tide swirls the sand and turquoise water into ever-changing patterns — is genuinely one of the most beautiful things on Earth. Take a seaplane or helicopter for the aerial view, or a boat trip from Airlie Beach. Stay on a sailing yacht overnight if you can. This is a must-do. Trust me on this one. — Fabian

Skydiving over Airlie Beach

If you're going to skydive anywhere, doing it over the Whitsundays with views of the islands, reef, and Whitehaven Beach below you is pretty hard to beat. Several operators run tandem jumps from 15,000 feet.

Gold Coast & Burleigh

Burleigh Heads & Gold Coast

Burleigh Heads is the Gold Coast's coolest neighbourhood — great surf, the Burleigh headland walk through rainforest, and a buzzing café and restaurant scene. The Gold Coast itself offers theme parks (if that's your thing), canal cruises, and the hinterland with Springbrook and Lamington National Parks.

South Australia

Adelaide & Hahndorf

Adelaide is a charming, walkable city with outstanding food and wine culture. The Central Market is a must. Just 25 minutes away is Hahndorf — Australia's oldest surviving German settlement, founded in 1839 by Lutheran refugees from Prussia. It's a surreal little taste of home for our German guests: German bakeries, beer halls, and a main street that could be in the Bavarian countryside. Try the Bienenstich at the German Cake Shop.

Barossa Valley

One of the world's great wine regions, just an hour from Adelaide. Famous for its bold Shiraz — some vines are over 150 years old, among the oldest in the world. Visit Penfolds, Henschke, and Seppeltsfield for outstanding tastings. The food scene is equally impressive, with farm-to-table restaurants throughout the valley.

McLaren Vale & d'Arenberg Cube

A stunning wine region south of Adelaide with beautiful rolling hills and over 80 cellar doors. The d'Arenberg Cube is the highlight — a striking, Rubik's Cube-shaped building housing a multi-level wine experience, a restaurant with panoramic views, and interactive art installations. The wines (especially the Shiraz and Grenache) are exceptional.

Kangaroo Island

Australia's Galápagos — a pristine island off the SA coast teeming with wildlife. See sea lions at Seal Bay, koalas in the wild, and remarkable rock formations at Remarkable Rocks. Allow at least 2 days. The local produce — honey, gin, oysters — is outstanding. Take the ferry from Cape Jervis.

Western Australia

Perth & Umgebung

Perth, Kings Park & Fremantle

Perth is a relaxed, sunny city with outstanding beaches. Kings Park offers sweeping views over the city and Swan River from its elevated botanic gardens. Take the train to Fremantle for its historic port, weekend markets (the Fremantle Markets are legendary), craft breweries, and excellent seafood at Bathers Beach House.

Rottnest Island & Quokkas

A car-free island paradise just 25 minutes by ferry from Fremantle. Rent a bike and cycle between pristine bays, snorkel in crystal-clear water, and meet the famous quokkas — the world's happiest animal. The quokka selfie is an Australian rite of passage. Allow a full day.

Pinnacles & Lancelin Sand Dunes

The Pinnacles Desert in Nambung National Park is otherworldly — thousands of limestone pillars rising from golden sand. Nearby Lancelin has massive white sand dunes perfect for sandboarding. Nothing beats tearing across sand on a BRP quad bike — ATV tours through the dunes are an absolute blast.

Margaret River

A world-class wine region about 3 hours south of Perth. Outstanding Cabernet Sauvignon and Chardonnay, plus stunning coastline, ancient caves (Jewel Cave, Mammoth Cave), and excellent surfing. The food and wine here rival anywhere in Australia.

WA Norden — Erweitertes Abenteuer

Für alle mit mehr Zeit: Die Küste von Western Australia nördlich von Perth gehört zu den spektakulärsten und am wenigsten besuchten Küstenabschnitten der Erde. Das ist wildes, unberührtes Australien vom Feinsten.

Ningaloo Reef

Australia's other great reef — and in many ways even better than the Great Barrier Reef because you can snorkel right from the shore. Swim with whale sharks (March–July), manta rays, and humpback whales. The coral is pristine and the marine life is staggering. Exmouth and Coral Bay are the main access points.

Coral Bay & Turquoise Bay

Coral Bay is a tiny, remote outpost with a beautiful reef just metres from the beach. Turquoise Bay in Cape Range National Park is regularly rated one of Australia's best beaches — the drift snorkel here, where the current carries you along pristine coral, is extraordinary.

Shark Bay & Shell Beach

A UNESCO World Heritage area with the famous Shell Beach — a beach made entirely of billions of tiny white shells, stretching for over 100km. Visit Monkey Mia for wild dolphin interactions, and see the ancient stromatolites at Hamelin Pool — some of Earth's oldest living organisms.

Hutt Lagoon Pink Lake

An extraordinary bubblegum-pink lake caused by algae and high salinity. The colour is most vivid in summer (around February — when you'll be here). Best viewed from above or from the roadside. It's surreal and utterly photogenic.

Tasmanien

Hobart & MONA

Tasmania's capital is compact, charming, and home to the extraordinary Museum of Old and New Art (MONA) — built into a sandstone cliff, housing a provocative and unforgettable private art collection. Take the MONA ferry there. The Salamanca Market (Saturdays) is one of Australia's best, and Hobart's food scene — especially fresh seafood — is outstanding.

Bruny Island & Get Shucked Oysters

A beautiful island south of Hobart accessible by a short ferry. The highlight is Get Shucked — an oyster farm where you sit by the water and eat freshly shucked oysters with a glass of Tasmanian wine. Also visit the Bruny Island Cheese Company and the stunning Neck viewpoint.

Wineglass Bay & Freycinet

One of the world's most photographed beaches. The walk to the Wineglass Bay Lookout takes about 45 minutes and reveals a perfect crescent of white sand and turquoise water surrounded by pink granite peaks. Continue down to the beach if you have time — it's worth it.

Bay of Fires

Named for the orange lichen on its granite boulders, the Bay of Fires is a series of stunning beaches along Tasmania's northeast coast. Crystal-clear water, white sand, and those extraordinary orange-red rocks. It's often deserted — a beach lover's dream.

Cradle Mountain & Dove Lake

The icon of Tasmania's wilderness. The walk around Dove Lake (6km, 2–3 hours) is one of the most scenic in Australia, with Cradle Mountain reflected in the still water. Come at dusk to spot wombats grazing on the boardwalks — they're wonderfully unfazed by people.

Northern Territory

Uluru Sunset & Field of Light

Uluru at sunset is one of the most powerful natural spectacles on Earth — the rock glows deep red, orange, and purple as the light changes. The Field of Light installation (by artist Bruce Munro) transforms the desert floor into 50,000 glowing stems at night. It's magical and moving in equal measure.

Base Walk & Kata Tjuta

Walk the 10km base trail around Uluru to understand its true scale and spiritual significance — the rock art, waterholes, and geological formations are extraordinary. Nearby Kata Tjuta (the Olgas) is equally stunning: the Valley of the Winds walk offers dramatic views through towering red domes.

Kings Canyon

The rim walk at Kings Canyon (6km loop) takes you along the edge of 100m-high sandstone walls with views over the vast desert landscape. The Garden of Eden — a permanent waterhole surrounded by lush vegetation — is a surreal oasis in the outback. Allow 3–4 hours for the full walk.

Darwin & Kakadu National Park

Darwin is a tropical frontier city with outstanding Asian-inspired food (the Mindil Beach Sunset Market is unmissable). Kakadu is Australia's largest national park — ancient Aboriginal rock art, dramatic waterfalls, and vast wetlands teeming with wildlife including saltwater crocodiles.

Hinweis: Februar ist Regenzeit im Top End. Erwartet Hitze, Feuchtigkeit und spektakuläre Nachmittagsgewitter. Einige Straßen und Gebiete können wegen Überschwemmungen gesperrt sein. Prüft die Bedingungen vor der Reise, aber die Wasserfälle sind dann am beeindruckendsten und die Landschaft üppig grün.

Neuseeland

Für alle, die den Sprung "über den Graben" wagen möchten — Neuseeland ist nur 3 Flugstunden von Sydney entfernt und bietet einige der dramatischsten Landschaften der Welt. Eine völlig andere Szenerie: schneebedeckte Berge, Fjorde, geothermische Wunder und sanfte grüne Hügel.

Nordinsel

Auckland & Waiheke Island

Auckland is New Zealand's largest city, set between two harbours. Take the ferry to Waiheke Island — a Mediterranean-feeling wine island with outstanding vineyards, olive groves, and beautiful beaches. Mudbrick and Cable Bay are standout wineries.

Hobbiton & Rotorua

The original Hobbiton movie set is preserved in the rolling green hills near Matamata — a must for any Lord of the Rings fan. Nearby Rotorua is the centre of Māori culture and geothermal activity: bubbling mud pools, geysers, and traditional hāngī feasts.

Tongariro Alpine Crossing

One of the world's great day walks — 19.4km across volcanic terrain past the emerald-coloured Emerald Lakes, through ancient lava flows, and along the rim of active craters. It's challenging but extraordinary. Allow 6–8 hours and check weather conditions carefully.

Südinsel

Queenstown

The adventure capital of the world, set on Lake Wakatipu with the Remarkables mountain range as a backdrop. Bungee jumping, skydiving, jet boating, and some of the best scenery you'll ever see. The food and wine scene is excellent too — the Gibbston Valley wineries are minutes away.

Milford Sound

A fiord of almost impossible beauty — towering cliffs rise vertically from black water, waterfalls cascade from above, and seals bask on the rocks. Take a boat cruise (essential) and, if you can, a scenic flight. The drive from Queenstown through the Homer Tunnel is an adventure in itself.

Wanaka & Roy's Peak

A quieter, more laid-back alternative to Queenstown on beautiful Lake Wanaka. The hike to Roy's Peak (1,578m) is one of the most rewarding in New Zealand — switchbacks up to a summit with 360-degree views of mountains and lakes. Allow 5–6 hours return.

Mount Cook & Hooker Valley

New Zealand's highest peak. The Hooker Valley Track is a 10km return walk through alpine scenery to a glacial lake at the foot of Mount Cook — swing bridges, icebergs, and raw mountain grandeur. One of the easiest and most rewarding walks in the country.

Kaikoura — Whale Watching

A coastal town on the South Island's east coast famous for year-round whale watching (giant sperm whales), swimming with dolphins, and crayfish so fresh it's unreal. The Kaikoura Peninsula Walkway offers dramatic mountain-meets-sea views.

Kulinarische Highlights in Neuseeland

Neuseelands Gastro-Szene überrascht mit Qualität. Probiert: Grünlippmuscheln (eine NZ-Spezialität), Lamm (eines der besten der Welt), Hāngī (traditionelles Māori-Festmahl im Erdofen), Pavlova (sie behaupten, es sei ihre Erfindung, nicht Australiens), L&P-Limonade (das Nationalgetränk) und den herausragenden Pinot Noir aus Central Otago.

Exotisches Australisches Essen

Australien hat eine einzigartig multikulturelle Gastro-Szene. Hier ist ein herausragendes Restaurant in jeder Großstadt, das etwas bietet, was ihr nirgendwo sonst findet.

Sydney — Nour

Modern Middle Eastern fine dining in Surry Hills. Chef Nelly Robinson creates extraordinary dishes using native Australian ingredients through a Lebanese lens. The wood-fired bread and mezze are exceptional.

Melbourne — Charcoal Lane

A social enterprise restaurant serving native Australian cuisine — kangaroo, emu, wattleseed, and bush tomato — prepared with contemporary flair. A meaningful and delicious dining experience.

Cairns — Ochre

Pioneer of Australian native cuisine. Try crocodile, barramundi, and dishes featuring bush herbs and spices you've never encountered. The tasting plate is a great introduction.

Adelaide — Africola

Bold, smoky African-inspired food with South Australian produce. Chef Duncan Welgemoed brings the flavours of his South African heritage to one of Adelaide's most exciting restaurants.

Perth — Wildflower

A rooftop restaurant at COMO The Treasury showcasing the six Noongar seasons through West Australian ingredients. The tasting menu is a journey through the landscape. Stunning views over Perth.

Packliste & Tipps

☀️

Sonnenschutz

Das ist nicht optional. Die UV-Strahlung in Australien ist extrem, auch an bewölkten Tagen. Packt LSF 50+ Sonnencreme ein (rifffreundlich beim Schnorcheln), einen Hut mit breiter Krempe, hochwertige Sonnenbrillen und ein UV-Shirt für Wasseraktivitäten. Alle 2 Stunden nachcremen.

💧

Flüssigkeitszufuhr

Immer Wasser mitnehmen, besonders im Sommer. Dehydrierung schleicht sich hier schnell ein. Bringt eine wiederverwendbare Wasserflasche mit — Leitungswasser ist in ganz Australien sicher und hervorragend. Im Outback mehr mitnehmen als ihr glaubt zu brauchen.

🏥

Reiseversicherung

Schließt eine umfassende Reiseversicherung ab, die medizinische Kosten (australische Gesundheitsversorgung ist teuer für Besucher) und Abenteueraktivitäten wie Fallschirmspringen, Jetski und Wandern abdeckt. Lest das Kleingedruckte zu spezifischen Aktivitätsausschlüssen.

💳

Bank & Karten

Informiert eure Bank und Kreditkartenanbieter vor der Abreise über eure Reise nach Australien. Karten werden fast überall akzeptiert — Bargeld braucht ihr selten. Überlegt euch eine Reisekreditkarte ohne Auslandseinsatzgebühren.

🔋

Powerbank

Bringt einen tragbaren Akku mit (20.000mAh empfohlen). Lange Tage beim Erkunden, Navigieren und Fotografieren entladen euer Handy schnell. Unverzichtbar für Tagesausflüge und abgelegene Gebiete.

🚗

Führerschein

Bringt euren Führerschein plus einen Internationalen Führerschein (IFP) mit. In Deutschland bekommt ihr ihn beim ADAC. Denkt daran: In Australien fährt man LINKS. Besondere Vorsicht an Kreisverkehren und Kreuzungen.

🚤

Bootsführerschein

Wer einen Sportbootführerschein hat, bringt ihn mit — Fabian nimmt euch vielleicht auf den Sea-Doos mit raus. Einige Bundesstaaten verlangen eine Lizenz für Jetskis.

🔌

Stromadapter

Australien nutzt Typ-I-Stecker (dreipolig). Bringt einen Universaladapter mit oder kauft einen Typ-I-Adapter am Flughafen. Die meisten Hotels haben USB-Ladeanschlüsse, aber verlasst euch nicht darauf.

📱

Nützliche Apps

TripView (Sydneys ÖPNV in Echtzeit), Google Maps (ladet Offline-Karten für Roadtrips und abgelegene Gebiete herunter), Uber/DiDi (Mitfahrdienst), Opal Travel (Fahrkarten-App).

📷

Wasserdichte Handyhülle

Unverzichtbar für Strandtage, Schnorcheln, Kajakfahren und Jetski. Eine gute wasserdichte Handytasche kostet unter $20 und bewahrt euch vor einem sehr teuren Unfall. Unbedingt einpacken.

Fortbewegung

Flüge: Qantas, Virgin Australia und Jetstar verbinden alle Großstädte. Frühzeitig buchen für die besten Preise — Jetstar ist die Budgetoption. Sydney nach Melbourne dauert ca. 1,5 Stunden, Sydney nach Cairns ca. 3 Stunden.

Autofahren: Es wird LINKS gefahren. Mietwagen sind weit verbreitet. Ein Internationaler Führerschein wird neben dem heimischen Führerschein empfohlen. Die Entfernungen sind riesig — Sydney nach Melbourne sind ca. 9 Stunden mit dem Auto.

Sydneys ÖPNV: Besorgt euch eine Opal Card (oder nutzt eine kontaktlose Kreditkarte) für Züge, Busse, Fähren und die Straßenbahn. Das Fährsystem ist eine der besten Arten, die Stadt zu erleben. Uber und DiDi sind überall verfügbar.
